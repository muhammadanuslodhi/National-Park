About Us

Since the 1960s, countries worldwide have established national parks to conserve biodiversity and cultural heritage. Governments, NGOs, and indigenous communities advocate for these protected areas, expanding networks globally. Management strategies have evolved, employing science-based approaches to preserve ecosystems, mitigate human impacts, and engage local communities.

Efforts to preserve cultural heritage within national parks often involve collaboration with indigenous communities, ensuring that their traditional knowledge and practices are respected and incorporated into management plans.

Governments and conservation organizations have increasingly recognized the importance of employing science-based approaches to manage these fragile ecosystems effectively. This has led to the implementation of comprehensive management plans aimed at preserving biodiversity, restoring degraded habitats, and mitigating the impacts of human activities such as habitat destruction and climate change. Additionally, there has been a greater emphasis on community engagement and collaboration, recognizing the vital role that local communities play in the long-term sustainability of national parks.

Safety Information: Stay informed about safety guidelines and regulations within the park. Important details such as emergency contact numbers, wildlife precautions, and park rules are clearly outlined on the map to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience for everyone.
